Part II: Red Alert Siren (Farnell Practical 35)

Star Trek TOS-style red alert using sawtooth + second harmonic, delay resonators, clipper distortion, and formant bandpass filters.

Signal chain: Saw (parabolic sweep 360→847Hz) + 2nd harmonic → HP@1Hz → 5 parallel delay resonators → 61ms loop → HP@12Hz → hard clip → 4 parallel BP filters (740, 1400, 1500, 1600Hz) → envelope


Bonus: Air Raid Siren

Classic WWII-style wailing siren that sweeps up and down continuously (~300Hz to ~1400Hz).

Signal chain: LFO (0.15Hz) → modulates Saw OSC frequency + 2nd harmonic → soft clipper → bandpass → highpass → output


Part II Writeup: Red Alert Siren

The sound I was aiming to mimic was the red alert on page 58 which was the original red alert from Star Trek series. This sound is a rising pitch sweep that cycles throughout. The synthesis techniques I used were the main sawtooth oscillator with a second sawtooth at a 2x frequency (I played around with the sound gain for this one but felt 0.3-0.4 was the best). The manual sweep was a bit tricky with it going from 360hz to 850hz over 900 ms (which can be seen at the bottom of the page at 641). If you look at the spectrum you can see two main regions of energy on page 643 and you can see these peaks exactly in figure 58.3. The synthesis I used was additive, waveshaping and subtractive. I used these because Farnell says "some kind of acoustic relaxation oscillator at the heart of the sound, maybe a type of diaphragm driven by an air blast" on page 644. The formant filters were good to recreate the clumps of energy. Some issues were using DFBE and its delay feedback, didn't sound true to the original sound.
